Research

Interdisciplinary research at the intersection of technology, policy, and society

Our Approach

The Technology Policy Lab conducts rigorous, interdisciplinary research that informs policy debates and shapes the future of technology governance. We collaborate across departments, bringing together expertise from computer science, law, public policy, ethics, and social sciences to tackle complex challenges.

Current Research Projects

AI Governance and Accountability Framework

Principal Investigators: Dr. Sarah Chen, Dr. Michael Roberts

This multi-year project examines how governments and organizations can develop effective governance frameworks for artificial intelligence systems. We are working with federal agencies to design accountability mechanisms that balance innovation with public safety and civil rights protection.

Funded by: National Science Foundation

Privacy-Preserving Data Sharing in Healthcare

Principal Investigators: Dr. Jennifer Lee, Dr. David Park

Investigating technical and policy approaches to enable secure health data sharing for research purposes while protecting patient privacy. This work includes developing frameworks for differential privacy implementation and analyzing HIPAA compliance in modern data ecosystems.

Funded by: Department of Health and Human Services

Digital Infrastructure and Rural Broadband Access

Principal Investigators: Dr. Maria Garcia, Dr. James Thompson

Examining barriers to broadband adoption in rural Virginia communities and evaluating the effectiveness of state and federal broadband expansion programs. Our research informs policy recommendations for closing the digital divide.

Funded by: Virginia Department of Housing and Community Development

Content Moderation and Platform Governance

Principal Investigators: Dr. Rachel Williams, Dr. Kevin Anderson

Analyzing how social media platforms make content moderation decisions and the implications for free speech, misinformation, and democratic discourse. This project includes comparative analysis of platform policies and regulatory approaches across different jurisdictions.

Funded by: Knight Foundation

Algorithmic Fairness in Criminal Justice

Principal Investigators: Dr. Omar Hassan, Dr. Lisa Martinez

Evaluating the use of algorithmic risk assessment tools in criminal justice settings, with particular attention to issues of bias, transparency, and due process. We are developing policy recommendations for jurisdictions considering adoption of these technologies.

Funded by: Arnold Ventures

Research Methods

Our research employs diverse methodologies to address complex policy questions:

Empirical Analysis

Quantitative and qualitative studies of technology adoption, policy implementation, and social impacts.

Technical Evaluation

Assessment of emerging technologies, security protocols, and system architectures.

Legal Analysis

Examination of regulatory frameworks, case law, and statutory interpretation.

Stakeholder Engagement

Collaborative work with government agencies, industry partners, and civil society organizations.
