Recent Publications

Algorithmic Accountability in the Age of AI: Designing Governance Frameworks for Public Sector Applications

Sarah Chen, Michael Roberts, Kevin Anderson

Journal of Technology Policy Studies, Vol. 47, No. 3 (2025)

This article examines the challenges of implementing effective accountability mechanisms for AI systems deployed in government settings. Drawing on case studies from federal and state agencies, we propose a multi-layered governance framework that addresses technical, organizational, and legal dimensions of algorithmic accountability. Our framework emphasizes the importance of documentation, audit trails, and human oversight in high-stakes decision-making contexts.

Privacy-Preserving Data Sharing: Technical Solutions and Policy Implications for Healthcare

Jennifer Lee, David Park, Tyler Washington

Health Affairs, Vol. 44, No. 2 (2025)

We analyze emerging privacy-preserving technologies, including differential privacy and secure multi-party computation, in the context of healthcare data sharing. Through interviews with health system administrators and privacy officers, we identify key barriers to adoption and propose policy reforms to facilitate responsible data sharing for research while maintaining patient confidentiality.

Bridging the Digital Divide: Evidence from Virginia's Rural Broadband Initiative

Maria Garcia, James Thompson, Nina Kim

Journal of Rural Studies, Vol. 93 (2024)

This empirical study evaluates the effectiveness of Virginia's rural broadband expansion programs from 2020-2024. Using a mixed-methods approach combining survey data, economic analysis, and qualitative interviews, we find that while infrastructure deployment has improved, adoption barriers related to affordability and digital literacy remain significant. We offer evidence-based recommendations for improving program design.

Content Moderation at Scale: Comparing Governance Approaches Across Social Media Platforms

Rachel Williams, Lisa Martinez

Information, Communication & Society, Vol. 28, No. 1 (2024)

We present a comparative analysis of content moderation policies and practices across major social media platforms, examining how platform governance structures, transparency mechanisms, and appeals processes vary. Our research reveals significant differences in approaches to harmful content and highlights implications for regulatory policy, particularly regarding platform accountability and user rights.

Bias and Fairness in Algorithmic Risk Assessment Tools: An Empirical Analysis of Criminal Justice Applications

Omar Hassan, Lisa Martinez, Emily Patel

Law and Society Review, Vol. 58, No. 4 (2024)

This study examines the use of algorithmic risk assessment tools in pre-trial detention and sentencing decisions across multiple jurisdictions. Through technical audits and analysis of case outcomes, we document patterns of disparate impact across demographic groups and evaluate the effectiveness of existing fairness mitigation strategies. We argue that technical solutions alone are insufficient and must be paired with robust procedural safeguards and human oversight.

The Privacy Paradox in Smart City Development: Policy Frameworks for Data Protection

David Park, Jennifer Lee, Chris Johnson

Urban Studies, Vol. 61, No. 5 (2024)

Smart city initiatives promise improved urban services but raise significant privacy concerns through pervasive data collection. We examine privacy policies and technical architectures across smart city deployments in North America and Europe, identifying best practices for privacy-by-design implementation. Our analysis informs recommendations for municipal governments seeking to balance innovation with privacy protection.

Policy Reports & White Papers

AI Governance for State and Local Governments: A Practical Guide

Technology Policy Lab

Policy Report (October 2025)

This comprehensive guide provides practical recommendations for state and local governments developing AI governance policies. Topics include procurement guidelines, impact assessments, transparency requirements, and accountability mechanisms. Includes case studies and model policy language.

Regulating Platform Competition: Lessons from International Approaches

Michael Roberts, James Thompson, Rachel Williams

White Paper (September 2025)

Comparative analysis of platform competition regulation in the European Union, United Kingdom, and United States. Evaluates different regulatory models and their effectiveness in promoting competition, innovation, and consumer welfare in digital markets.

Data Privacy and National Security: Finding the Balance

Jennifer Lee, Michael Roberts, Omar Hassan

Policy Report (June 2025)

Examines tensions between data privacy protections and national security imperatives in the context of encryption, data localization, and cross-border data transfers. Proposes framework for policy-makers to evaluate trade-offs and design balanced approaches.

Working Papers

Explainability Requirements for AI Systems in Government: Technical Feasibility and Policy Design

Sarah Chen, Emily Patel, Kevin Anderson

Working Paper (2025)

Explores the technical capabilities and limitations of current explainability methods for machine learning systems. Analyzes how explainability requirements can be effectively incorporated into procurement and deployment policies for government AI systems.

The Economics of Data Portability: Implications for Competition and Innovation

James Thompson, Chris Johnson

Working Paper (2025)

Economic analysis of data portability requirements as a tool for promoting competition in digital markets. Examines potential effects on consumer switching costs, network effects, and innovation incentives.
