About the Lab

Building bridges between technology innovation and responsible policy-making

Our Mission

The University of Virginia Technology Policy Lab was founded to address the growing need for rigorous, interdisciplinary research at the intersection of technology and policy. We bring together scholars, practitioners, and policymakers to tackle the most pressing challenges posed by emerging technologies.

Our work spans artificial intelligence governance, data privacy, digital infrastructure, platform regulation, and algorithmic fairness. Through empirical research, technical analysis, and stakeholder engagement, we generate actionable insights that inform policy decisions at local, state, and federal levels.

What We Do

Research

We conduct cutting-edge research that advances understanding of technology's societal impacts and informs evidence-based policy solutions.

Education

We train the next generation of technology policy leaders through graduate fellowships, undergraduate research programs, and professional development opportunities.

Engagement

We facilitate dialogue between academia, government, industry, and civil society through workshops, seminars, and collaborative research projects.

Policy Impact

We translate research findings into practical policy recommendations, working directly with government agencies and legislative bodies.

Our Approach

The Technology Policy Lab takes an interdisciplinary approach to research, drawing on expertise from computer science, law, public policy, economics, philosophy, and social sciences. We believe that effective technology policy requires both technical understanding and careful consideration of social, ethical, and legal implications.

Our research is characterized by:

  • Empirical rigor: We employ robust methodologies including quantitative analysis, qualitative research, and technical evaluation.
  • Practical relevance: Our work addresses real-world policy challenges and generates actionable recommendations.
  • Stakeholder collaboration: We engage with diverse perspectives from government, industry, civil society, and affected communities.
  • Ethical commitment: We consider the distributional impacts of technology policies and prioritize equity and justice.

Programs & Opportunities

Graduate Research Fellowships

We offer competitive fellowships for PhD students and law students conducting research on technology policy topics. Fellows receive funding support, mentorship from faculty affiliates, and opportunities to collaborate on lab projects. Applications are accepted annually in January.

Undergraduate Research Program

Undergraduates can work as research assistants on lab projects, gaining hands-on experience in policy research and analysis. Positions are available during the academic year and summer. We particularly encourage applications from students in computer science, data science, public policy, and related fields.

Tech Policy Discussion Series

Our weekly discussion series brings together students, faculty, and visiting experts to explore current issues in technology policy. Sessions cover recent policy developments, emerging research, and career pathways in the field. Open to the UVA community.

Visiting Scholars Program

We host visiting scholars from academia, government, and industry for short-term research collaborations. Visitors contribute to lab projects, present their work, and engage with the UVA community. Inquiries welcome on a rolling basis.
